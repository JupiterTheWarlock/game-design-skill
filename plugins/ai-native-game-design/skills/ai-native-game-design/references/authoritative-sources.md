# Sources and applicability notes

Use this file to verify provenance and scope. It is a source index, not a claim
that every working category or design hypothesis applies to every game.

## Practitioner evidence

- Tencent Game Institute, “AI原生游戏正在重新理解玩法丨初光EP.01全回顾.”
  <https://gameinstitute.qq.com/news/detail/317>

- Tencent Game Institute, “当 AI 学会‘记住’和‘回应’丨初光EP.02全回顾,”
  published July 21, 2026.
  <https://mp.weixin.qq.com/s/KBCZWEY1lVvvFvfhq_3Pvw>

The EP.01 edited panel recap features Lü Xin of the Communication University
of China Digital Human Research Institute, Fang Ke of Tsinghua SIGS
AgentLand, and independent AI-game creator Zang Tianpei. It reports
practitioner taxonomies, prototype lessons, and hypotheses about AI-native
gameplay, bounded input, runtime adjudication, character continuity,
playtesting, and retention.

The EP.02 recap features Liu Han, producer of *The Planet Crafter*, and Thomas,
the system-design lead for *Game for Peace*. It reports two practitioner paths
for AI NPCs—a shared population ecology and a close companion—plus project
observations about authored social structure, narrative milestones, layered
memory and reflection, scene-relative intelligence, output review,
out-of-character behavior, and offline NPC activity.

Treat both recaps as practitioner evidence, not as peer-reviewed research,
industry standards, or proof of market adoption, universal genre suitability,
current model capability, player psychology, memory effectiveness, emotional
realism, or retention effects. Do not generalize EP.02's project-specific model
sizes, subjective quality scores, deployment scale, or observed model behavior
into universal requirements.

The repository-authored `ai-native-game-design.md` and `ai-npc-design.md`
synthesize reusable design questions from the panels without copying either
article or its images. Preserve competing definitions and design paths when a
project does not fit one category cleanly, and validate every project-specific
recommendation through evidence.
